brown butter caramel biscotti

brown butter biscotti with caramel bits

recipe adapted from Barley & Sage


baked in January 2026

yield: 36 biscotti
prep time: 10 minutes
cook time: 50 minutes
difficulty level: easy



image from Barley & Sage

Ingredients
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted brown butter, room temperature
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract
  • 2 cups all purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup almond flour, substitute all purpose flour
  • 1 and 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 cup Kraft caramel squares, cut into eighths

Directions
  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.
  2. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment (or a large bowl with a hand mixer), beat together the room temperature brown butter, sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
  3. Add in the eggs, vanilla, and almond extract and beat until just combined.
  4. Add in the flour, almond flour, baking powder, and salt and mix until fully incorporated.
  5. Then stir in the caramel bits. The dough should be sticky.
  6. Scoop half the dough onto one side of baking sheet and pat it into a 10 inch x 2 inch rectangle that’s about 1 inch thick (lightly wetting your hands helps a lot with this step). Repeat with the other half of the dough.
  7. Place the baking sheet with the shaped biscotti logs in the fridge and chill for about 20 minutes (this helps them not spread too much).

  8. While the biscotti logs are chilling, preheat the oven to 350°F.
  9. Bake at 350°F for 20 to 30 minutes, until the top and edges are set. Remove from the oven and let cool for 25 to 30 minutes.
  10. Reduce the oven temperature to 325°F.
  11. Carefully transfer the logs to a cutting board and use a spray bottle filled with room-temperature water to very lightly spritz them (just one spray on each section of the logs). Wait about 5 minutes before using a very sharp serrated knife to slice biscotti 1/2 inch wide.
  12. Return the slices to the baking sheet, standing them up with 1/2 inch in between them, so air can circulate around them. Bake for another 25 to 30 minutes, until dry and golden.
  13. Remove from the oven and transfer to a wire rack to cool.
