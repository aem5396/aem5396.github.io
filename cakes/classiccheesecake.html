classic cheesecake

classic creamy cheesecake with graham cracker crust and strawberry topping

recipe from Sally's Baking Addiction


baked in July 2022

yield: 12 servings
prep time: 45 minutes
cook time: 1 hour
difficulty level: medium

Ingredients

Crust

  • 1 and 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs (about 10 full sheet graham crackers)
  • 5 Tablespoons (5/8 stick) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar

Filling

  • four 8-ounce blocks full-fat cream cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup full-fat sour cream, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons fresh lemon juice (optional, but recommended)
  • 3 large eggs, at room temperature

Topping

  • Homemade Strawberry Sauce

Directions

For the crust:

  1. Adjust the oven rack to the lower-middle position and preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Using a food processor, pulse the graham crackers into crumbs.
  3. Pour into a medium bowl and stir in sugar and melted butter until combined. Mixture will be sandy.
  4. Press firmly into the bottom and slightly up the sides of a 9-inch or 10-inch springform pan. No need to grease the pan first. Use the bottom of a measuring cup to pack the crust down tightly.
  5. Pre-bake for 8 minutes.
  6. Remove from the oven and place the hot pan on a large piece of aluminum foil. The foil will wrap around the pan for the water bath.
  7. Allow crust to slightly cool as you prepare the filling.

For the filling:

  1. Using a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the cream cheese and granulated sugar together on medium-high speed in a large bowl until the mixture is smooth and creamy, about 2 minutes.
  2. Add the sour cream, vanilla extract, and lemon juice then beat until fully combined.
  3. On medium speed, add the eggs one at a time, beating after each addition until just blended. After the final egg is incorporated into the batter, stop mixing. To help prevent the cheesecake from deflating and cracking as it cools, avoid over-mixing the batter as best you can. You will have close to 6 cups of batter.
  4. Boil a pot of water. You need 1 inch of water in your roasting pan for the water bath, so make sure you boil enough.
  5. As the water is heating up, wrap the aluminum foil around the springform pan.
  6. Pour the cheesecake batter on top of the crust. Use a rubber spatula or spoon to smooth it into an even layer.
  7. Place the pan inside of a large roasting pan.
  8. Carefully pour the hot water inside of the pan and place in the oven. (Or you can place the roasting pan in the oven first, then pour the hot water in. Whichever is easier for you.)
  9. Bake cheesecake for 55-70 minutes or until the center is almost set. If you notice the cheesecake browning too quickly on top, tent it with aluminum foil halfway through baking. When it’s done, the center of the cheesecake will slightly wobble if you gently shake the pan.
  10. Turn the oven off and open the oven door slightly. Let the cheesecake sit in the oven in the water bath as it cools down for 1 hour.
  11. Remove from the oven and water bath, then cool cheesecake completely uncovered at room temperature.
  12. Then cover and refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  13. Use a knife to loosen the chilled cheesecake from the rim of the springform pan, then remove the rim.
  14. Using a clean sharp knife, cut into slices for serving. For neat slices, wipe the knife clean and dip into warm water between each slice.
  15. Serve cheesecake with homemade strawberry topping and whipped cream.
